Sight loss

Sight loss is perhaps one of the most inconvenient disabilities. In a world where we are reliant on visual cues for many daily tasks, such as texting, working, or even just a walk in the streets, those who are visually impaired suffer the most. The lack of adaptations for these people also prove a problem: tools such as tactile paving are not yet widespread. It is therefore no surprise that the visually impaired are much more susceptible to accidents and injuries than those who are not. 40% of visually impaired people state that they have collided with an aerial or suspended obstacle at least once every year, and a whopping 95% of them state that they have collided with an obstacle at least once (Chang, Chen, Sie and Yang, 2021).


We, people who do not suffer from visual impairments, built the world as it is today. It is our responsibility, as fellow humans, to help change it so it supports those who are visually impaired as well. One method of doing so is utilising technology.


I may not be an engineer, but I hope I can offer parts of a solution in this CBA. Had this been suggested before, then let my project be another voice to encourage its development.
